Programme 1: Models and Simulation Methods Development




    Modelling and simulation solves real world problems without any disruption or disturbances to the real world system. It is more efficient and cost effective means to determine the impact of a variable change in the system. The analysis can be verified through scientific support and easily translated to real world system. Modelling and simulation plays a role across all industries and disciplines by providing insights into the functioning of complex systems. Locally developed tools and solutions are envisaged to be deployed in manufacturing, chemicals, energy, and mining industries across the country. Direct economic impacts for industry include savings on the costs of experiments, faster turnaround time on the development of new complex and integrated systems, increase in employment rate and income. The programme was born out of the initiative to display the benefits of modelling and simulation. As a first iteration of two projects are under development,namely:

    · Project 1: Engineering Sciences Models Development

    . Project 2: Economics Sciences Models Development



    Programme 2: Mult Phenomena Multiscale Modelling and Simulation Technologies




    Mult phenomena multiscale modelling and simulation technologies encompass a variety of approaches aimed at capturing complex systems' behaviour across multiple scales. This includes discrete-event systems models, which focus on events occurring at distinct points in time, Agent-based Simulation Models, which simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents, and continuous modelling and simulation, which deal with systems evolving over continuous time and space. Integration of these diverse modelling and simulation techniques is crucial for comprehensively understanding and predicting system behaviour. Various integration models exist, such as hybrid approaches combining discrete-event and continuous models, or frameworks that incorporate agent-based models within larger-scale simulations. These integration models enable us to tackle multifaceted problems and gain insights into systems characterized by diverse phenomena and scales.


    • Project 1: Modelling and Simulation of Discrete Event Systems

    • Model Verification and Validation : Model verification and validation are the key processes for quantifying and establishing credibility in numerical models and simulations. The academic institution and industries require better modelling tools for model verification and validation of products performance, including validated models and verified simulation methods. Also, availability of more structured and validated information is essential for the ability to reliably simulate options and make sound data-based business decisions. Model validation ensures that the physics implemented in the model is assessed by checking accuracy of the prediction against experimental measurements whereas model verification ensures that the model is correctly implemented by checking model inputs and outputs with simplified example cases. This project will focus on the development of standards and plans for model verification and validation, to promote the development of high-fidelity models in various fields.

    Programme 4:Data-Driven Modelling and Simulation Technologies




    • >Data Mining :The availability of huge volumes of data along with advancements in computational resources and data analytics are expected to have a revolutionary impact on modelling, simulation and decision making. Better use of existing data as well as availability of more standardised, structured, and validated information is fast becoming essential for the ability to reliably simulate options and make sound data-based business decisions. The vast amounts of data readily available in the water, energy, mining, tourism, and anti-corruption industries make these ideal target markets for application and use of data-driven decision and support tools. The envisaged methods will facilitate the model-based integration of a variety of datasets, critical assessment of the available information, and tailored selection of future data-driven experiments. The so-called hybrid approaches where physics-driven and data-driven modelling and simulation are combined will soon become more important. On the other hand, there is an opportunity for data-driven modelling and simulation to be further enhanced to become a valuable source of data in the ‘big data’ realm. As such, data-driven modelling and simulation technology is becoming an integral part of a digital R&D organisation.
